Calculating BMR at Calorie Tracker

At Calorie Tracker, we have studied methods and formulas to calculate the BMR for our clients. The Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) Calculator estimates your basal metabolic rateâ€”the amount of energy expended while at rest in a neutrally temperate environment, and in a post-absorptive state (meaning that the digestive system is inactive, which requires about 12 hours of fasting). However, we understand that calorie intake varies from person to person based on activity levels. For this reason, we use an external calculator that takes our client information and then compares it to certain activity levels which we can discuss with our client. The formulas below are the source of how these estimates are found! These BMR findings for the client are then shown on the summary page, which is compared to the daily calorie count tracked by our tracking algorithm.

Calorie Tracker For Men

Mifflin-St Jeor Equation : 10W + 6.25H - 5A + 5
Harris-Benedict Equation : 13.397W + 4.799H - 5.677A + 88.362

Calorie Tracker For Women

Mifflin-St Jeor Equation : 10W + 6.25H - 5A - 161
Harris-Benedict Equation : 9.247W + 3.098H - 4.330A + 447.593 ** NOTE ** :
W = Weight in kilograms (kg)
H = Height in centimeters (cm)
A = Age
